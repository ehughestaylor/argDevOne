Why Atlantic County



Atlantic County attracts more than 25 million visitors each year in addition to 10,000 second homeowners. From the excitement of Atlantic City’s casinos with world class dining, entertainment and shopping, to the beauty of our scenic rivers and wildlife refuges, there is something of interest for all ages and abilities. Our quality education institutions and healthcare facilities further enhance this extraordinary quality of life.

Higher Education

Atlantic County is home to a nationally recognized university, a comprehensive community college, a blue ribbon technical high school, and outstanding public, private and charter schools.

  • Stockton University

    Located in Galloway Township, is one of America’s most distinctive public universities of the arts, sciences, and professional studies. Ranked at #14 among 2017’s Top Public Regional Universities of the North by U.S. News & World Report, Stockton’s expansion plans serve as an economic driver for the region. The new Stockton University campus in Atlantic City plans to open in 2018 with about 1,000 students and grow from here

  • Atlantic Cape Community College

    Expanded its facilities with a new $44 million state-of-the-art Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics building, student center and a Hospitality Center. Its Casino Career Institute and Academy of Culinary Arts are both critically acclaimed. In addition, the college secured a $2 million Economic Development Administration grant towards the construction of an Aviation Institute which will help provide a trained workforce to further strengthen the aviation industry in the county.

  • Atlantic County Institute of Technology

    is the most comprehensive technical education facility in Atlantic County. Situated on a 58-acre campus in the Hamilton Business Park in Mays Landing, the Institute of Technology offers students an innovative curriculum, which combines academic instruction, career technical education, internships, and structured work experiences designed to bridge the gap between classroom learning and workplace skills.

  • Beaches and Boardwalk

    Atlantic County boasts being one of the top destination spots for families along the Jersey Shore. From upscale beaches to world-famous entertainment, there’s something for everyone to enjoy along this small slice of the east coast. The Atlantic City beaches are famous, and rightly so. Everything you could possible want is right here within walking distance, from shops to five-star restaurants to casinos, attractions and great shows. If you want to enjoy the fun Atlantic City--minus the crowds and noise--Brigantine, Margate, Longport, and Ventnor City offer a family-friendly atmosphere just a short drive from the rides and entertainment of the Atlantic City boardwalk.

  • Shopping

    From the Tanger Outlet, The Walk in Atlantic City, to the boutique shops of Historic Mays Landing and downtown Hammonton, Atlantic County provides residents and visitors alike the very best retail experience available. On the world-famous Atlantic City Boardwalk, you’ll find AC staples like salt-water taffy or delicious fudge, souvenir stores, and specialty shops. Whether its high-end retail options or discounted brand-name fashions Atlantic County has it all and everything in between.

  • Casinos and Gambling

    It may come as no surprise that one of Atlantic City’s greatest draws is its casinos. Atlantic City gaming action comes in virtually every variety. From high-stakes poker table to penny slots there’s something fun for everyone.

  • Dining

    Home to wildly-extensive restaurant options, from well-known chain eateies to celebrity-chef destinations powered by the likes of Wolfgang Puck, Bobby Flay, Marc Forgione and Geoffrey Zakarian, it’s all on the menu in Atlantic County. The Atlantic City dining scene is so popular, there’s even an annual celebration of it every spring during Atlantic City Restaurant Week.

  • Arts & Culture

    Atlantic County is abound with a selection of arts and culture. The proof is in the art galleries, museums, wineries, and cultural events. Located in the heart of Downtown Hammonton is the Arts District which includes The Stockton College Noyes Museum and the Hammonton Arts Center both features fine arts, such as painting and sculpting and classes and workshops while the historic Eagle Theatre hosts professional and community performances every weekend.
